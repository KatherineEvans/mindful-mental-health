About Me

Hello! My name is Emily Schwass (she/her), and I am a Licensed Clinical Social Worker-Supervisor. I hold a Master's Degree in Social Work from the University of St. Francis in Illinois and a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Sociology with a concentration in Social Welfare from Augustana College in Illinois. I have been dedicated to social work since 2013. Originally from Chicago suburbs, I have been living in Texas since 2017.

Throughout my career, I have gained extensive experience working with individuals of all ages, in a variety of settings, including crisis intervention, college counseling center, community mental health, partial hospitalization program, and inpatient treatment center.

I believe in a comprehensive and integrative approach to therapy, blending several ideas and techniques to best meet each client's unique needs. I utilize a range of evidence-based therapeutic interventions, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT), mindfulness-based interventions, and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT).

My practice is grounded in a social justice lens, recognizing that mental health is profoundly influenced by social, cultural, and political factors. My role is to create a space where you feel empowered to make choices and take actions that are aligned with your true self. Your voice will always be central in our sessions, and your autonomy will be respected and nurtured. I focus on your inherent strengths and capabilities, rather than just your challenges. You are the expert of your own life, and our sessions will be guided by your experiences, needs, and goals.

In my free time, I enjoy taking care of my plants, reading, re-watching my favorite tv shows, being outdoors, and spending time with my family, friends, and my dog, Marlee.

I look forward to the opportunity to work with you. Please do not hesitate to reach out with any questions you may have. Together, we can navigate the complexities of life with compassion, understanding, and strength.
