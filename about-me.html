About Me

My name is Emily Schwass. I am a Licensed Clinical Social Worker. I have a Master’s Degree in Social Work, from University of St. Francis in Illinois and a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Sociology with a concentration on Social Welfare from Augustana College in Illinois. I have been a social worker since 2015.

My experience includes working with children and adolescents in mental health crises and adults with serious mental illness. I have experience working with depression, anxiety, adjustment disorders, early onset psychosis, stress management, and self-esteem. I have worked in community mental health centers, partial hospitalization program, and inpatient psychiatric hospital.

I believe in taking a strength based and client centered approach, utilizing techniques such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT), along with other evidenced based therapeutic methodologies.

It is my passion to support my client in obtaining the best possible life. Everyone has a unique story, and it takes strength and courage to share that story. I strive to create a safe, non-judgmental environment. It is important to me that you feel comfortable to openly express and explore your thoughts, feelings, and experiences. I want you to feel empowered to take control of your life and be your best self. I am originally from Illinois and have lived in Texas since 2017. In my free time, I enjoy spending time with my family and friends, and my two dogs, Walter and Marlee. I love being outdoors, even in this Texas heat! Being in nature is where I find solace. I look forward to working with you. Please do not hesitate to reach out with any questions you may have.
